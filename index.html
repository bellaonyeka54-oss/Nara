The Problem

A preventable disease is killing women in silence.

Cervical cancer is almost 100% preventable \u2014 yet it is the leading cause of cancer death among women in Sub-Saharan Africa. The reason is not biology. It is access.

\ud83c\udfe5

No Screening Access

Most Nigerian women have never visited a gynaecologist. Clinics are expensive, far away, and culturally intimidating. Screening simply does not happen.

\ud83d\udd07

No Awareness

Women do not know what symptoms to watch for, what cervical cancer is, or that it can be caught early. Knowledge that could save their lives simply does not reach them.

\ud83d\udcf1

Existing Apps Miss This User

Apps like Flo and Eve were built for women who already have healthcare access. They track periods and ovulation. They were never designed for the unscreened Nigerian woman.

\ud83d\udde3\ufe0f

Language Barriers

Health information in Nigeria is almost entirely in English. Women who speak Pidgin, Yoruba, Igbo or Hausa are left behind by every existing solution.

How Nara Works

Three steps from symptoms to care.

Nara meets women exactly where they are \u2014 with no registration, no cost, and no judgment.

1

Tell Nara How You Feel

Describe your symptoms freely in any language. Nara listens like a trusted friend and asks gentle follow-up questions to understand your situation.

2

Get Your Risk Assessment

Nara's AI assesses your cervical cancer risk level and explains clearly what your symptoms might mean in simple, plain language \u2014 no medical jargon.

3

Find a Clinic Near You

Nara connects you to the nearest verified, affordable screening clinic in Nigeria \u2014 with contact details, costs, and a reminder to actually go.
